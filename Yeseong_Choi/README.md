## Abstract

Ab initio multiple spawning (AIMS) describes nonadiabatic dynamics with a basis of frozen Gaussians that is spawned near regions of nonadiabatic coupling. Because each basis function is a single frozen Gaussian, converging the population transfer through a conical intersection (CI) requires a large basis and many initial conditions, and even then transfer away from the CI is hard to capture. We extend AIMS by dressing each moving Gaussian with ladder operator excitations, so that the Hermite structure generated by the coupling is represented inside a frame rather than by additional spawns — one excited frame does the work of a dense cluster of spawned Gaussians. Spawning handles the transfer near the crossing (CI), where population builds up and separates onto the acceptor surface, while the ladder operators handle the transfer elsewhere, where the coupling only dresses the frame with co-moving excitations. On the four-mode pyrazine model, benchmarked against exact MCTDH, the method converges to the exact diabatic populations with a minimal basis of N=183 (maximum error 0.178) — about 2× more accurate at ~40% of the basis of adiabatic AIMS (N=465, error 0.329).

## 1. Motivation

Nonadiabatic dynamics at conical intersections (CI) is critically important, and there is a need for methods that describe it both accurately and efficiently. 

Single frozen Gaussian is exact for the dynamics on a harmonic potential, but it breaks down in two cases:
- Nonadiabatic coupling(adiabatic picture) or Electronic coupling(diabatic picture).
- Anharmonic potential.

Ab initio multiple spawning (AIMS) tackles this by spawning basis functions. However:
- Large basis is required to capture the population near the CI, many initial conditions (Wigner sampling) still fall short.
- AIMS spawns only where the NAC is large, missing transfer on anharmonic regions of the PES.

## 2. Introducing ladder operators

Regard a frozen Gaussian as the ground state $|0(q,p)\rangle$. Applying the raising operator generates excited states $|1(q,p)\rangle$, $|2(q,p)\rangle$, $\cdots$ at the same center.

$$
|n(q,p)\rangle = c\left(\hat{a}^\dagger\right)^n |0(q,p)\rangle
$$

<br>

**Why ladder operators?**

**(i) $|n(q,p)\rangle$ is a finite difference of $\{|0(q_i,p_i)\rangle\}$.** With the displacement operator $\hat{D}(\alpha)$,

$$
\hat{a}^{\dagger}|0\rangle = \partial_\alpha \hat{D}(\alpha)|0\rangle\big|_{\alpha=0}, \qquad |n\rangle = \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{n!}}\,\partial_\alpha^{\,n}\!\big(\hat{D}(\alpha)|0\rangle\big)\big|_{\alpha=0}
$$

so level $n$ is the $n$-th derivative (finite difference) of a displaced Gaussian. **One $|n(q,p)\rangle$ captures inside a single frame what a dense cluster of spawns would.**

**(ii) Diabatic picture — linear electronic coupling is captured exactly.**

$$
\hat{V} = c\,\hat{q} \propto \hat{a}^\dagger + \hat{a}
$$

**(iii) Adiabatic picture — the nonadiabaticity operator contains ladder operators.**

$$
\hat{\Theta}_{ab} = \frac{1}{2M}\left( 2\langle\phi_a|\nabla_n|\phi_b\rangle\,\nabla_n + \langle\phi_a|\nabla^2_n|\phi_b\rangle \right), \qquad \nabla_n \sim \hat{a}^\dagger - \hat{a}
$$

Also, near the CI the PES is anharmonic.

<br>

**Consequence.** Ladder operators capture the Hermite structure of the Hamiltonian without spawning ⇒ the same accuracy is reached with fewer spawns and a smaller basis. 

Therefore:
- Instead of one Gaussian per frame, we load a set of ground + excited state (excited Gaussians up to level $K$ per mode) as the basis.
- They share the same center and width, move together along the classical trajectory, and their coefficients are propagated with the full-overlap variational metric.

## 3. Why the adiabatic picture fails

However, ladder operators break the locality of the Gaussians. If ladder operators are used, the adiabatic picture is no longer usable.

AIMS uses the saddle-point (centroid) approximation:

$$
\langle\chi_j|\hat{V}|\chi_k\rangle \simeq V(\bar{q}_{jk})\,\langle\chi_j|\chi_k\rangle
$$

- Once locality is lost, this approximation breaks down. 
- So we must evaluate $\langle\chi_j|\hat{V}|\chi_k\rangle$ over regions where the NAC acts. 
- In the adiabatic picture the NAC diverges there. 
- The matrix element cannot be computed. 

**Use the diabatic picture.**
- On the fly: local diabatization with a local harmonic approximation (LHA).
- In the diabatic picture the coupling is smooth (linear in $q$), so the matrix element stays finite (indeed analytic) even for delocalized ladder states.

## 4. Spawning in the diabatic picture

Then, how do we spawn in the diabatic picture?
- In the diabatic picture, electronic transitions occur *everywhere*; spawning at every point would make the basis far too large.
- Transitions are strongest near the crossing of the diabatic surfaces (≈ the conical intersection). 

So we split the roles:
- Transitions near the crossing → handled by **spawning**.
- Transitions elsewhere → handled by the **ladder operators**.

A few details:
- Giving every frame $\{|0(q_i,p_i)\rangle\}$ the same excitation level $K$ is wasteful --- the acceptor frames use a slightly lower excitation.
- With many modes the number of excitation combinations explodes $(K_1 \times K_2 \times \cdots)$, so we keep only a selected subset.

## 5. Results

### Computational details
- We use the four-mode pyrazine model from the Heidelberg MCTDH examples (`pyrmod4.op`); bright state S2, T=0, 120 fs.
- Exact populations: Heidelberg **MCTDH** (`pyr4.inp`, multi-set).
- AIMS populations: **pySpawn17** in the adiabatic representation, then converted to diabatic populations.
- Ladder-operator populations: our own python code (`tower_pyr4.py`) in the diabatic representation.

### Populations and convergence

| method | representation | basis N | max\|err\| vs MCTDH |
|--------|----------------|--------:|--------------------:|
| **MCTDH** (exact) | diabatic | (grid) | — (reference) |
| ladder — full | diabatic | 657 | **0.164** |
| ladder — cutoff + CI-gate | diabatic | **183** | **0.178** |
| ladder — low excitation | diabatic | 65 | 0.371 |
| **AIMS** | adiabatic | 465 | **0.329** |

The detailed graphs can be found in `slides_yschoi.pdf`.

## 6. Summary and future plans

**Summary.**
- Extending AIMS with ladder operators and formulating it in the diabatic representation yields a compact basis that converges to the exact result (MCTDH) at the four-mode pyrazine model.
- Efficiency: Our method is ~2× more accurate at ~40% of the AIMS basis (183 vs 465) (0.178 vs 0.329).
- AIMS plateau: The AIMS error does not fall below ~0.33 regardless of the number of ICs or the spawn threshold — a structural limit of the single frozen Gaussian.


**Future plans.**
- Anharmonic potentials.
- On-the-fly (ab initio) dynamics.
- Squeezed basis functions ⇒ back to the adiabatic picture.

# Reproducing the benchmark

Regenerates the full four-mode pyrazine benchmark from scratch (no pre-computed data): the exact MCTDH reference, pySpawn AIMS, and our diabatic ladder-operator methods.

## One command
```bash
sbatch run_all.sbatch     # SLURM;  writes EVERYTHING to ./data/
#   or, without a scheduler (any machine):
bash run_all_local.sh     # LOCAL
```
All generated data go into `data/`. Please edit the hard-coded program paths in the scripts to point to your own installation.

## Pipeline
1. MCTDH 
- `mctdh86 -mnd -w pyr4.inp` → `data/pyr4/output`
- `analyze_mctdh.py` → `data/mctdh_pop.csv` 
2. Ladder operator 
- `tower_pyr4.py` (self-contained, numpy only)
- full N=657 (cutoff=5, no gate) → `data/swarm_pop_pyrmod4.csv`
- near CI spawn → `data/ci_combo/`
3. AIMS(pySpawn17) 
- `start.py i` for i=0..95 → `data/ic??/sim.hdf5`
4. Coherent projection 
- `analyze_aims.py` → `data/aims_pop.csv`, `data/aims_perrun.npz`

## `tower_pyr4.py` — command-line options

| flag | default | varies across the 3 runs? | meaning |
|------|---------|---------------------------|---------|
| `--Kp=6,3,3,3` | `6,3,3,3` | **yes** (k4 uses `4,2,2,2`) | per-mode excitation levels K_p (modes v10a, v6a, v1, v9a) |
| `--cutoff=5` | full basis | **yes** (5 / 4 / 2) | config selection: keep multi-indices with Σ n_k ≤ cutoff |
| `--spawnkp=3,2,2,2` | `=--Kp` | **yes** (k4 `2,1,1,1`) | excitation levels for the *spawned* acceptor frames |
| `--spawngap=0.5` | `0` (off) | **yes** (off / off / 0.3–0.5) | CI-gate: only spawn where the diabatic gap \|ΔE(x)\| < g |
| `--tag=pyrmod4` | `pyrmod4` | **yes** (output name) | output file tag → `swarm_pop_<tag>.csv` |
| `--spawn=5` | `5.0` | no | spawn interval (fs) |
| `--merge=0.8` | `0` (off) | no | merge two same-state frames when their overlap > thr |
| `--prune=1e-3` | `0` (off) | no | drop a frame once its norm < eps |
| `--pruneage=15` | `2.0` | no | minimum frame age (fs) before it may be pruned |
| `--reg=1e-6` | `1e-8` | no | overlap-metric eigenvalue floor (S⁻¹ regularisation) |
| `--tf=120` | `120.0` | no | final time (fs) |
| `--dt=0.1` | `0.1` | no | integration substep (fs) |


The three pipeline invocations (all share `--spawn=5 --merge=0.8 --prune=1e-3 --pruneage=15 --reg=1e-6 --tf=120 --dt=0.1`):
- **full N=657** — `--Kp=6,3,3,3 --cutoff=5 --spawnkp=3,2,2,2 --tag=pyrmod4`
- **N=65** — `--Kp=4,2,2,2 --cutoff=4 --spawnkp=2,1,1,1 --tag=k4`
- **N=111/159/183** — `--Kp=6,3,3,3 --cutoff=2 --spawnkp=3,2,2,2 --spawngap=0.3|0.4|0.5 --tag=c2g<g>`
